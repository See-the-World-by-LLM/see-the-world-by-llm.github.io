Aswan, Egypt - A fairytale city by the Nile, an explosion of history, and a treat for any foodie. From ancient temples to stunning mosques, and from spicy curries to sweet dates, this hidden gem has something for everyone.

A Playful Welcome

Aswan, Egypt. Welcome to the land of eternal blue sky and timeless beauty. If you're about to visit this enchanting city, we hope you're ready for a treat that's a mix of ancient culture, modern-day charm, and some old-school hospitality.

At Least 3 Fun Facts About Aswan

  • The city's name comes from the Phoenician word for 'lily', symbolizing the city's sacred connection to the ancient Egyptian goddess Isis.
  • Aswan is home to the stunning Temple of Isis on Philae Island, one of the most impressive architectural wonders in Egypt.
  • The city's iconic white and blue buildings are made from around 70% limestone, a unique blend of Roman and Egyptian architectural styles.

Local Food You Must Try

When in Aswan, you need to try some of the local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Machboos al-Ful, a Pilaf of rice, meat, and spices that's a classic part of Egyptian cuisine.
  • Koshari, a hearty vegetarian dish made with pasta, lentils, tomato sauce, and fried onions.
  • Shish Taouk, a popular Egyptian dish made with marinated chicken skewers served with a side of pita bread and salad.

A One-Day Itinerary

If you only have 24 hours in Aswan, here's a suggested itinerary for you:

Morning

  • Start your day with a visit to the Temple of Isis on Philae Island. This stunning temple is a must-see and a sacred place for ancient Egyptians.
  • Take a short boat ride across the Nile to the nearby village of Deir el-Bahri. This ancient city is home to some of the most impressive tombs in Egypt, including the tomb of Pharaoh Ramses II.

Afternoon

  • Head to the bustling Souk of Aswan, where you can find everything from traditional Egyptian clothing to handmade crafts and souvenirs.
  • Visit the magnificent Mosque of Darb al-Ahmar, one of the most beautiful mosques in Egypt.

Evening

  • End your day with a traditional Egyptian dinner at a local restaurant, where you can sample the local cuisine and enjoy some live music and dancing.

Expectation vs. Reality (Humorous Comparison)

Believe it or not, Aswan might not live up to your expectations. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • The city's narrow alleys can be quite crowded, making it feel like a bustling market.
  • The ancient temples might not be as impressive as you expect, but trust us, they're still breathtaking.
  • Don't be fooled by the city's poverty; Aswan is home to some of the most beautiful and historic sites in Egypt.
