A Playful Welcome

Welcome to Perm, Where Quirky Meets Cool! Tucked away in the Ural Mountains, Perm is not your average Russian city. It's a place where you'll find museums dedicated to locomotives, grand Soviet architecture, and some of the friendliest locals you'll ever meet. So, grab your sense of adventure (and a warm coat) and get ready to explore one of Russia's best-kept secrets!

Fun Facts About Perm

  • ** Perm is home to the last known remains of the woolly mammoth.** Yes, you read that right! The Perm State Museum has an impressive collection of Ice Age relics, including a 40,000-year-old mammoth skull.
  • The city has its own "Perm-36" prison museum. This former Soviet labor camp turned museum offers a sobering glimpse into the country's turbulent past. Just don't expect a five-star review from past "guests."
  • Perm is the gateway to the "Rock City" of Kungur. Located just a short train ride away, Kungur's stunning ice caves are a natural wonder that will leave you breathless.

Local Food You Must Try

  • Pelmeni: These Russian dumplings are a staple in Perm, typically served with butter, sour cream, and a side of dill. Pro tip: Eat them with your hands for an authentic experience!
  • Blini with Caviar: Thin, delicate pancakes smothered in sour cream, caviar, and a sprinkle of chives. It's a taste sensation that's sure to impress your taste buds (and your Instagram followers).
  • Pirozhki: These flaky pastries are filled with everything from meat and onions to fish and mushrooms. Perfect for a quick, delicious snack on the go!

Expectation vs. Reality

  • Expectation: A dull, industrial city with nothing to see or do.
  • Reality: A vibrant, eclectic city with a thriving arts scene, rich history, and some of the best food you'll find in Russia.
  • Expectation: Locals who are as cold as the Siberian winters.
  • Reality: Friendly, welcoming residents who are eager to practice their English and share their love for Perm.
  • Expectation: A complicated, frustrating experience navigating the city.
  • Reality: A surprisingly easy-to-explore city with helpful signage, affordable taxis, and a compact downtown area.
